Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It completes an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days, but a full rotation takes 59 Earth days. The planet has a cratered surface, similar to the Moon, and experiences extreme temperature changes, ranging from 430°C during the day to -180°C at night. Despite its size, Mercury has a weak magnetic field. It has no moons or rings and has been visited by spacecraft like Mariner 10 and MESSENGER, with BepiColombo set to arrive in 2025.

About Mercury

Smallest planet and closest to the Sun.
Orbit: 88 Earth days to orbit the Sun.
Day length: 59 Earth days for one full rotation.
Surface: Covered in craters; no significant atmosphere.
Temperature extremes: 430°C (day) to -180°C (night).
Magnetic field: Present but weak.
Exploration: Mariner 10, MESSENGER, and upcoming BepiColombo.
Size: Slightly larger than Earth’s Moon.
