Space Mission Overview

Mission Details

Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission):

Launch Date: November 5, 2013 Launch Vehicle: PSLV-C25 (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) Mission Objective: To explore Mars and study its surface features, morphology, atmosphere, and mineralogy from orbit. Mission Duration: Initially intended to last six months, but the spacecraft far exceeded its expected lifespan, functioning for over seven years, until communication was lost in September 2022. Arrival at Mars: Mangalyaan successfully entered Mars' orbit on September 24, 2014. Achievements: It made India the first Asian country to reach Mars' orbit. Mangalyaan sent back important data, including high-resolution images of Mars' surface, helping scientists study the Martian atmosphere and weather patterns. It was one of the most cost-effective interplanetary missions ever, with a total cost of approximately $74 million USD. The spacecraft carried five scientific instruments, including: A Mars Color Camera (MCC) to capture images of Mars and its moons. A Methane Sensor for Mars (MSM) to detect methane in the Martian atmosphere, which is considered a possible indicator of life. A Thermal Infrared Imaging Spectrometer (TIS) to study surface composition and mineral distribution. Name: Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan. Objective: To explore Mars' surface, atmosphere, mineralogy, and morphology. Cost: Around $74 million USD, making it one of the most economical space missions to Mars. Unique Achievement: India became the first nation to successfully reach Mars on its maiden attempt, a remarkable feat as only NASA, the Soviet Union, and the European Space Agency had previously done so. Instruments on Board: Mars Color Camera (MCC): Captured stunning images of Mars. Lyman Alpha Photometer (LAP): Studied the abundance of deuterium and hydrogen in the Martian upper atmosphere. Methane Sensor for Mars (MSM): Searched for methane, a potential indicator of life. Thermal Infrared Imaging Spectrometer (TIS): Analyzed Mars' surface composition. Mars Exospheric Neutral Composition Analyzer (MENCA): Measured neutral particles in the Martian atmosphere.
