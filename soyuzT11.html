Space Mission Overview

Mission Details

1. Launch Date: April 3, 1984 2. Mission Duration: 7 days, 21 hours, 40 minutes 3. Launch Vehicle: Soyuz-U The Soyuz-U rocket launched the Soyuz T-11 spacecraft into space from the Baikonur Cosmodrome in Kazakhstan. 4. Mission Objectives: The primary objectives of the Soyuz T-11 mission were: To transport a crew to the Salyut 7 space station. To conduct scientific experiments in space, with a particular focus on material science, Earth observation, and biomedical studies. International Cooperation: This mission was notable for the collaboration between India and the Soviet Union, marking a milestone in international space cooperation. 5. Spacecraft: Soyuz T-11: This was a variant of the Soyuz spacecraft, designed to ferry cosmonauts to space stations like Salyut 7. It carried three crew members to the Salyut 7 station and docked successfully with it. 6. Space Station: Salyut 7: The destination for the Soyuz T-11 mission. Salyut 7 was the last of the Soviet Union’s Salyut space stations and was used primarily for scientific research and testing space technologies. Soyuz T-11 Crew: Yuri Malyshev (Soviet Union) – Commander Role: Malyshev was the commander of the Soyuz T-11 mission, responsible for piloting the spacecraft and ensuring a successful docking with the Salyut 7 station. Background: Malyshev was a veteran Soviet cosmonaut with experience in long-duration spaceflight. Gennady Strekalov (Soviet Union) – Flight Engineer Role: Strekalov was responsible for technical and engineering tasks aboard Soyuz T-11 and the Salyut 7 space station.
